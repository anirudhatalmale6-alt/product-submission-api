<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b1b97f73c0434963f168bb8fc22cf942" id="how-to-choose-the-right-cat-bed" style="color:#111111;margin-bottom:30px;font-size:32px;font-style:normal;font-weight:700;line-height:1.2">How to Choose the Right Cat Bed</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-6bc7bbf192b4e5524724758ad2835ccb wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#4a4a4a;margin-top:16px;margin-bottom:16px;font-size:18px">Choosing the right cat bed comes down to your cat’s age, size, sleeping style, and any joint or health issues. A well-chosen bed can improve their sleep quality, protect their joints, and keep them comfortable all year round.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:group -->
<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;background-color:white;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-cfb5546b6694e357813825bf4a079456" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">📏 Size &amp; Fit</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-7f97e67dfab0bda19129a717fae92afb wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Getting the right size is one of the most important steps. A cat bed that’s too small will be ignored, and one that’s too big may not feel cosy enough for cats that prefer snug spaces.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-dfb732df99d63b86b686f87029759d47" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>Measure your cat <strong>from nose to base of tail</strong> while lying stretched out, then add 20–25 cm.</li>
<li>For <strong>donut or cave beds</strong>, the interior diameter should match this measurement.</li>
<li>Large breeds like <strong>Maine Coons</strong> need XL beds — always check internal dimensions.</li>
<li>In multi-cat homes, provide <strong>one bed per cat plus one extra</strong> to prevent resource guarding.</li>
<li><strong>Kittens</strong> need small, enclosed cat beds — they lose body heat quickly and crave security.</li>
<li>If your cat <strong>changes sleeping position</strong> often, choose a bed slightly larger than the stretched-out measurement to accommodate all postures.</li>
</ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
</div>
<!-- /wp:group -->

<!-- wp:group -->
<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;background-color:white;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-fc1461d9a2e525b9f4f986ba7142daa8" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🧵 Material &amp; Fabric</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-58722ef6535b991d6d78455f3f1451a1 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">The material affects warmth, durability, ease of cleaning, and how attractive the bed is to your cat. Different cats have strong tactile preferences.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b2e1c7354cbfdd30db45fd102c4a18fc" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li><strong>Fleece &amp; Sherpa:</strong> Soft, warm, affordable, and machine washable — a great all-rounder.</li>
<li><strong>Plush &amp; Velvet:</strong> Premium feel, attractive in modern interiors, irresistible for kneading cats.</li>
<li><strong>Linen &amp; Cotton:</strong> Breathable and ideal for warmer months or centrally heated homes.</li>
<li><strong>Memory Foam:</strong> Exceptional joint support for senior cats — with removable washable covers.</li>
<li><strong>Seasonal rotation:</strong> Many owners keep two beds — a breathable cotton one for summer and a thermal-lined one for winter.</li>
<li><strong>Anxious cats:</strong> Soft, plush fabrics with raised edges or hoods help nervous cats feel safe and enclosed.</li>
</ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
</div>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;background-color:white;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-fb81bed5ee808008f6c6308076bc547c" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">📍 Placement &amp; Location</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b7ac9f3b9dcc185c053cb778fa59b95f wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Even the most perfectly chosen bed will go unused if placed somewhere your cat finds unsuitable. Cats are particular about their sleeping environments.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-6c5ab02cdbfb941b3d8f69ff29f7f119" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>Place beds in <strong>warm, quiet corners</strong> away from high-traffic areas and draughts.</li>
<li>Near <strong>radiators or sunny windowsills</strong> are consistently popular positions.</li>
<li>Many cats prefer <strong>elevated spots</strong> — on bookshelves, cat trees, or sturdy furniture.</li>
<li>In multi-storey homes, place a bed on <strong>each floor</strong> — especially for senior cats.</li>
<li>For <strong>shy or anxious cats</strong>, place the bed in a low-traffic corner or inside a closet with the door ajar — enclosed, quiet locations help them feel secure.</li>
<li>Observe your cat&#8217;s <strong>natural sleeping spots</strong> for a few days — then place the bed in their preferred location for the best chance of adoption.</li>
</ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
</div>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;background-color:white;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-0f8cf109fbcebc7426dcbf17b8daa6b4" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🧹 Cleaning &amp; Maintenance</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-8ffae57ce155d566f646854eed979d8d wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Regular cleaning is essential for your cat’s health and the hygiene of your home. Unwashed beds accumulate fur, dander, and bacteria.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-c7fb0d126c6299f3a4f16cc7fd4cdafd" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>Choose beds with <strong>removable, machine-washable covers</strong> for easy maintenance.</li>
<li>Wash at <strong>40°C every 2–3 weeks</strong> with a pet-safe detergent.</li>
<li><strong>Shake outdoors</strong> and lint-roll before washing to prevent clogging the machine.</li>
<li>Replace beds when padding <strong>flattens or loses support</strong> — typically every 1–3 years.</li>
<li>For <strong>senior or arthritic cats</strong>, choose orthopaedic beds with waterproof liners — accidents become more common with age, and the liner protects expensive memory foam.</li>
<li>Rotating between <strong>two beds</strong> extends each bed&#8217;s lifespan and ensures your cat always has a clean option available.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-left alignwide has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-f56e1de3345327f09c3a69e5b72ef0a6" id="cat-beds-faq" style="color:#1a1a1a;margin-bottom:25px;font-size:32px;font-style:normal;font-weight:700;line-height:1.2">Cat Beds FAQ</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:details -->
<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px">
<summary>Why does my cat ignore their new cat bed?</summary>
<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is one of the most common frustrations among cat owners. Cats are creatures of habit and strong olfactory preference, and a new cat bed that smells of the factory, packaging, or another animal may simply be unappealing at first. To encourage your cat to use new cat beds, place an item of your worn clothing inside or nearby to introduce a familiar scent. You can also sprinkle a small amount of catnip on or near the bed, or move the bed to a location you’ve observed your cat already gravitating towards. Give it at least two to three weeks before concluding your cat won’t use the bed — patience is key.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</details>
<!-- /wp:details -->

<!-- wp:details -->
<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px">
<summary>How many cat beds does my cat need?</summary>
<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The standard recommendation is one cat bed per cat, plus one extra. For a single-cat household, this means two cat beds — one primary sleeping spot and one alternative so the cat can choose based on their mood. In practice, many cats naturally gravitate towards three or four favourite sleeping spots around the home, so having cat beds available in each of those areas helps redirect them away from your furniture. In multi-cat homes, having more cat beds than cats is strongly advised to prevent resource competition.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</details>
<!-- /wp:details -->

<!-- wp:details -->
<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px">
<summary>Are heated cat beds safe?</summary>
<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When purchased from reputable brands and used correctly, heated cat beds are entirely safe. Self-warming cat beds that use reflective materials rather than electricity carry no electrical risk and are safe to leave out unsupervised. Electric heated cat beds should always bear a recognised UK safety mark and include automatic temperature regulation to prevent overheating. Never use a human heating pad as a substitute for proper heated cat beds, as these are not designed for animals and can cause serious burns. Always provide your cat with the option to move away from a heated bed if they become too warm.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</details>
<!-- /wp:details -->

<!-- wp:details -->
<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px">
<summary>Should I let my cat sleep in my bed or get them their own cat beds?</summary>
<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is a personal choice, and both approaches are valid. Many cat owners enjoy the companionship of their cat sleeping alongside them, and research suggests this can strengthen the human-animal bond. However, having dedicated cat beds available gives your cat an alternative sleeping option and means you have a fallback if you prefer an undisturbed night. It’s also worth noting that cats are crepuscular and highly active in the early morning hours, which can disrupt human sleep. Providing comfortable cat beds in the bedroom — on a chair or at the foot of the bed — can satisfy your cat’s desire to be near you while giving you the option of boundaries.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</details>
<!-- /wp:details -->

<!-- wp:details -->
<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px">
<summary>How long do cat beds typically last?</summary>
<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The lifespan of cat beds varies considerably by quality and construction. Budget fleece cat beds that are washed frequently can begin to pill, lose shape, or lose their insulating properties within six to twelve months. Mid-range cat beds with quality construction and removable washable covers typically last two to four years. Premium orthopaedic cat beds with high-density foam can last five years or more, though the fabric cover may need replacing sooner. Signs that cat beds need replacing include flattened or lumpy padding that no longer provides support, permanent odours that washing cannot remove, or visible wear such as torn seams or exposed foam.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</details>
<!-- /wp:details -->

<!-- wp:details -->
<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px">
<summary>Can I make my own cat beds?</summary>
<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Absolutely. DIY cat beds are a popular option for crafty owners, and a simple sewn cushion cover filled with polyester stuffing or old fabric scraps can make a perfectly functional resting spot your cat may adore. Repurposed items such as old suitcases lined with a cushion, wooden crates with a mat insert, or cardboard boxes with fleece blankets all make creative and cost-effective cat beds. The main considerations are safety — avoid materials with loose threads, toxic dyes, or parts that could be ingested — and washability, as homemade cat beds still need to be regularly cleaned to stay hygienic.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</details>
<!-- /wp:details -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="cat-beds-maintenance" style="font-size:28px">How to Clean and Maintain Cat Beds</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Keeping your cat beds clean is essential for your cat’s health and your home’s hygiene. Most quality cat beds come with removable, machine-washable covers — look for this feature when buying cat beds, as it makes maintenance much easier.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Wash your cat beds at least once a fortnight, or weekly if you have multiple cats or outdoor cats. Use a pet-safe detergent and avoid fabric softeners, which can irritate sensitive skin. Between washes, vacuum your cat beds regularly to remove hair, dander, and dirt.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Inspect your cat beds monthly for signs of wear. Flat spots, lumpy filling, or worn-through fabric means it’s time for new cat beds. Most quality cat beds last 1–4 years depending on use. Rotating between two cat beds can extend their lifespan significantly.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="cat-beds-mistakes" style="font-size:28px">5 Common Mistakes When Buying Cat Beds</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The biggest mistake people make with cat beds is choosing the wrong size. Cat beds that are too small leave your cat cramped and uncomfortable, while oversized cat beds can make cats feel exposed and insecure. Always measure before buying cat beds.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Another common error is prioritising style over comfort when choosing cat beds. While it’s nice to have cat beds that match your décor, your cat’s comfort should always come first. Also, don’t place cat beds in high-traffic areas or near draughts — cats need a quiet, warm spot to rest. Ignoring your cat’s sleeping preferences is another mistake: observe whether they curl up, stretch out, or hide before choosing a bed style.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="seasonal-bedding" style="font-size:28px">Seasonal Bedding Tips for Year-Round Comfort</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Your cat’s sleeping needs change with the seasons. During summer months, breathable cotton mats, elevated perches, and mesh-based window beds help prevent overheating. Look for moisture-wicking fabrics and ventilated designs that promote airflow around your sleeping cat.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Winter calls for insulated, plush options with thermal linings or self-warming materials. Placing the sleeping area away from draughty windows and cold floors makes a significant difference to your cat’s comfort during colder months.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Regardless of season, the sleeping surface should always be appropriate for your cat’s weight and sleeping position. Side sleepers need wider, flatter surfaces, while cats that curl up prefer donut or nest-style options with supportive raised edges.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Waterproof liners are a worthwhile investment year-round. They protect the filling from accidents and damp fur. Most quality liners are machine-washable and add an extra layer of comfort while extending the life of your cat’s sleeping area.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Multi-cat households should provide each animal with their own dedicated resting space. Sharing can lead to resource guarding and stress. Position sleeping areas in separate quiet spots where each cat feels secure and undisturbed.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="toc-placement-tips">Placement Tips for Your Cat’s Sleeping Area</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Where you position your cat’s resting spot matters as much as the product itself. Choose a quiet corner away from high-traffic doorways, draughts, and direct sunlight. A consistent location helps your cat associate that area with rest, making settling down at night or during the day far easier. If your home has multiple levels, consider placing a sleeping option on each floor so your cat always has a comfortable retreat nearby.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Temperature regulation plays a role in placement. During winter, move the sleeping area away from external walls and windows that radiate cold. In summer, tile or wood floors stay cooler than carpeted rooms, making them ideal locations. Window perches with mesh bases allow air circulation, which is particularly welcome for thick-coated breeds during warmer months.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For households with multiple cats, each animal should have their own designated resting space. Sharing can lead to resource guarding and disturbed sleep. Place sleeping areas within sight of each other if your cats are bonded, but far enough apart that each animal feels they have personal territory. This arrangement promotes harmony and ensures every companion gets the quality rest they need from their cat beds.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Matching the resting surface to your cat’s sleeping style improves comfort dramatically. Cats who curl up prefer donut or bolstered options that cradle their body. Cats who sprawl flat need a wide, unconstrained cushion. Side sleepers benefit from a low bolster on one edge that supports the neck without restricting movement. Observing how your cat naturally settles reveals which design will deliver the most restful and restorative sleep for their individual preference.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
