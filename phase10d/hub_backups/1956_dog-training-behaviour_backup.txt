<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-left alignwide has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-85ced15dc353f59a16f83de8ce30fe83" id="dog-training-faq" style="color:#1a1a1a;margin-bottom:25px;font-size:32px;font-style:normal;font-weight:700;line-height:1.2">Dog Training FAQ</h2>

<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px"><summary><strong>When should I start training my puppy?</strong></summary>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">You can begin basic training as early as 8 weeks old. Start with simple commands like sit and name recognition, keeping sessions short (5-10 minutes) and always positive.</p>
</details>

<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px"><summary><strong>What is positive reinforcement training?</strong></summary>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Positive reinforcement means rewarding your dog for desired behaviour with treats, praise, or play. It&#8217;s the most effective and humane training method, recommended by veterinary behaviourists worldwide.</p>
</details>

<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px"><summary><strong>How long does it take to train a dog?</strong></summary>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Basic commands can be learned in a few weeks with consistent daily practice. More complex behaviours may take months. Every dog learns at their own pace — patience and consistency are key.</p>
</details>

<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px"><summary><strong>My dog pulls on the lead — what should I do?</strong></summary>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Use a front-clip harness and practice the &#8216;stop and wait&#8217; technique — stop walking whenever your dog pulls and only continue when the lead is loose. Reward them for walking beside you.</p>
</details>

<details class="wp-block-details alignwide has-border-color has-ast-global-color-4-background-color has-background is-layout-flow wp-block-details-is-layout-flow" style="border-color:#e5e5e5;border-width:8px;margin-bottom:12px;padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;font-size:18px"><summary><strong>Do I need a professional trainer?</strong></summary>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Many owners successfully train their dogs at home using online resources and quality equipment. However, a professional trainer can help with specific behavioural issues like aggression, separation anxiety, or reactivity.</p>
</details>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-21e494c1e1f1cda054b6a134b92805f5" id="how-to-choose-the-right-equipment" style="color:#111111;margin-bottom:30px;font-size:32px;font-style:normal;font-weight:700;line-height:1.2">How to Choose the Right Equipment</h2>

<p class="has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b385efc7033ad87c095e9b8c3ac1ff46 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#4a4a4a;margin-top:16px;margin-bottom:16px;font-size:18px">Choosing the right training equipment depends on your dog&#8217;s size, temperament, and training goals. The right combination of tools ensures a safe, effective, and enjoyable experience for both of you.</p>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-white-background-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b9e28dc7e44a06c28dd61126cef144c9" id="dog-training-essentials" style="color:#111111;margin-bottom:30px;font-size:32px;font-style:normal;font-weight:700;line-height:1.2">Dog Training Essentials</h2>

<p class="has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-8f813f33ab713f74a3e001075e55092f wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#4a4a4a;margin-top:16px;margin-bottom:24px;font-size:18px">From basic commands to solving common behaviour challenges, these expert-backed training cards give you everything you need to raise a well-mannered, happy dog.</p>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-white-background-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-a84c35bfa15546d1d4a9f4cfb27098f5" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🎓 Dog Training Fundamentals and Key Commands</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-fdd67c92f12dd5f4146225a5ecc70a06 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Successful dog training begins with understanding how dogs learn. Positive reinforcement — rewarding good behaviour with treats, praise, or play — is the most effective approach backed by modern science. Every training programme should start with five foundation commands that keep your dog safe in everyday situations.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-2eda38805cce907851c5ee3e5c40c4af" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>Use positive reinforcement (treats, praise, play) rather than punishment-based methods that can damage your bond</li>

<li>Keep sessions short and frequent — aim for 5–10 minutes, 3–4 times per day, to maintain your dog’s focus</li>

<li>Ensure every family member uses the same commands and follows the same rules to avoid mixed signals</li>

<li>Master the five essentials first: sit, stay, come, down, and leave it — these form the building blocks for all advanced training</li>

<li>Practise the ‘come’ recall in a quiet, enclosed space before adding distance and distractions — a reliable recall can save your dog’s life</li>

<li>Train ‘leave it’ regularly with increasing temptations so your dog learns to ignore dangerous items on walks</li>

<li>End every session on a successful note, even if you need to simplify the task, to build confidence and enthusiasm</li>

</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-5ed88a97ff3e3f05d83efae78e932cdb" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🧰 Must-Have Dog Training Tools and Equipment</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-593e9fe78729b9248ba0641e720eb52c wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">The right equipment makes training more effective and enjoyable. From treat pouches to long lines, investing in quality tools creates a clear communication bridge between you and your dog.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-7613b0184d438125475d177837fa8c2d" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>A treat pouch keeps high-value rewards (cheese, chicken, or commercial treats) accessible during every session</li>

<li>Choose tiny treats so your dog doesn’t fill up — small, soft pieces allow rapid-fire repetitions</li>

<li>A clicker marks the exact moment your dog performs the desired behaviour, creating precise communication</li>

<li>If a clicker isn’t your style, pick a short, consistent verbal marker word and use it the same way every time</li>

<li>Long lines (5–10 metres) are invaluable for recall practice and loose-lead walking in open spaces</li>

<li>Always attach a long line to a well-fitted harness rather than a collar to protect your dog’s neck during training exercises</li>

<li>A front-clip harness combined with the ‘be a tree’ method significantly reduces lead pulling within weeks</li>

</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-c2802e457666acd2b8d1686675058a2f" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">📅 Puppy Training Timeline: What to Teach and When</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-a08d0aaebe2cf00306588ec6862a212d wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Puppies develop rapidly, and timing your training to each developmental stage sets the foundation for lifelong good behaviour. Here is a stage-by-stage guide to building skills at the right pace.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-4b447e86ce3a9b1a8c427adc51fd26ef" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>Weeks 8–12: Focus on socialisation and name recognition — expose your puppy to different people, sounds, surfaces, and environments in positive ways</li>

<li>Months 3–4: Introduce sit, stay, and come in 3–5 minute sessions with high-value rewards; toilet training should follow a consistent routine after meals, naps, and play</li>

<li>Months 4–6: Build on basics with ‘leave it’, ‘drop it’, and loose-lead walking as your puppy’s attention span grows enough for 10-minute sessions</li>

<li>Months 6–12: Stay patient through adolescence — hormones may make your pup seem to forget everything, but consistent reinforcement through this crucial phase establishes lifelong habits</li>

<li>Beyond 12 months: Continue reinforcing good behaviours and introduce advanced skills like agility, scent work, or trick training to keep your dog mentally stimulated</li>

<li>The critical socialisation window (8–16 weeks) shapes your puppy’s confidence and temperament for life — make every new experience a positive one</li>

</ul>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-white-background-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-dacd3189e7e7f7f65137001f4ae554da" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🐕 Common Behaviour Challenges and Solutions</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-bf1ebfac208567cd13f75f43fe2b047c wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Even well-trained dogs develop challenging behaviours. Understanding the root cause helps you apply the right solution — most issues respond well to patient, consistent redirection rather than punishment.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-5ac21a541b1a93d6eb55293b8440cd3c" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>Jumping up: completely ignore the behaviour (turn away, cross arms, no eye contact) and only reward four paws on the ground — ensure every person your dog meets follows the same rule</li>

<li>Lead pulling: use the ‘be a tree’ method — stop completely when the lead goes taut and move forward only when it goes slack</li>

<li>Barking at visitors: teach a ‘place’ command where your dog goes to a designated spot when the doorbell rings, and reward calm behaviour generously</li>

<li>Anxiety-based barking: gradual desensitisation to the trigger, paired with high-value rewards, produces lasting results without increasing stress</li>

<li>Counter-surfing or stealing food: manage the environment first (clear surfaces), then train a reliable ‘leave it’ and reward your dog for choosing to stay on the floor</li>

<li>Separation anxiety: build alone-time tolerance gradually, starting with seconds and increasing duration — never punish anxious behaviour</li>

</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-613e62e4204bf58600ff1763686d8674" id="useful-tips-for-every-pet-owner" style="color:#111111;margin-bottom:30px;font-size:32px;font-style:normal;font-weight:700;line-height:1.2">Useful Tips for Every Pet Owner</h2>

<p class="has-text-align-center has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-13c11de5a5f8135a59c66ba31f9eabef wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#4a4a4a;margin-top:16px;margin-bottom:24px;font-size:18px">Whether you are a first-time owner or an experienced handler, these practical tips will help you build a stronger bond with your dog through training, environmental awareness, and daily care.</p>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-white-background-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-bde6d1c4150d3e72023612335aec30fc" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🌍 Adapting Training to Different Environments</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-43a604757607d8fd3a202d98c927e4ea wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Skills learned in the living room do not automatically transfer to the park, the street, or a friend&#8217;s house. Practising commands in varied settings builds reliability and teaches your dog to focus despite distractions.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-f283eb633e5d7d56d4da1f469909b3fe" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>Start in low-stimulus environments and gradually increase the challenge — a quiet garden is easier than a busy park, which is easier than a pet-friendly shop</li>

<li>Practise on different surfaces (wet grass, gravel, sand, paving) to normalise unfamiliar terrain and build confidence</li>

<li>Controlled introductions to new people, animals, bicycles, pushchairs, and loud vehicles prevent reactive behaviour</li>

<li>Group training classes provide structured social encounters with professional oversight that catches emerging issues early</li>

<li>Combine solo practice with group sessions to develop both obedience skills and a well-rounded temperament</li>

<li>Always carry high-value rewards in new environments — unfamiliar settings need stronger motivation than your living room</li>

</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-83fac74b06d65fe93aa893af21b67e24" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">📝 Consistency, Patience, and Tracking Progress</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-18dcff1dd5c120c99b42e6d0cdb11350 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">The most underrated tools in any training programme are patience and consistency. Rushed sessions lead to frustration, while steady, recorded progress keeps both you and your dog motivated for the long journey ahead.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-ba8ece9f23abcd1c7a0200556ca57751" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>Agree on a shared set of commands, reward criteria, and boundaries with every household member before you begin</li>

<li>Post written house rules in a visible spot so everyone gives your dog the same clear guidance regardless of who holds the lead</li>

<li>Keep a simple training journal noting date, commands practised, success rate, and distractions present for objective progress tracking</li>

<li>End every session on a successful repetition — even if you need to simplify the task — to build your dog&#8217;s confidence and eagerness</li>

<li>Review your journal weekly; many owners are surprised to find their dog&#8217;s reliability has improved significantly even when daily sessions feel slow</li>

<li>Quality always outweighs quantity — five focused minutes beat thirty distracted ones when building lasting behavioural habits</li>

</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-8558a2a39a661f9887cff871537b3fb1" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">💡 Everyday Habits That Strengthen Your Bond</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b4dfd2ba349c3b5302be72a364838f53 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Training does not stop when the formal session ends. The way you interact with your dog throughout the day reinforces the behaviours you want to see and deepens the trust between you.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-a4c953921a374fdcebf92e33b53ab38b" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">

<li>Use meal times as mini training sessions — ask for a sit or a stay before placing the bowl down</li>

<li>Reward calm behaviour spontaneously throughout the day, not just during dedicated training windows</li>

<li>Rotate toys and enrichment puzzles weekly to keep your dog mentally stimulated and prevent boredom-based misbehaviour</li>

<li>Incorporate short training cues into daily walks — practise recalls, direction changes, and sit-stays at kerbs</li>

<li>Groom your dog regularly; brushing and handling exercises build tolerance for vet visits and reduce stress</li>

<li>Spend at least 15 minutes of quiet, focused time with your dog each day — simply being present strengthens your relationship</li>

</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b0768d8fe5fd92d8fa6611959326cede" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🎯 Start with the Basics</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-39c765b46da1ceb1d182de2d6cc49080 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">A quality training lead, treat pouch, and clicker are the foundation of any training toolkit.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-8d6b67a4a7e83ea066f0d8f1a8669697" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>Start with the basics: a quality training lead, treat pouch, and clicker</li>

<li>Choose treats that are small, soft, and high-value — your dog should be excited to earn them</li>

<li>A treat pouch keeps rewards accessible and your hands free during sessions</li>

<li>A standard 1.8m lead is ideal for heel work and basic obedience</li>

<li>Box clickers are louder outdoors; button clickers are better for sensitive dogs</li>
</ul>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-3d2fe8dac10f10cd5068e1f052a5321c wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:1px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Having the right basics from the start prevents frustration and helps you focus on building good habits rather than fighting poor equipment. Invest in quality items that will last through months of daily training sessions.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-38500a1c524c5f92585902fbcc0a4d34" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🐾 Choose the Right Harness</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-36cdd7543648b275bcfac4c6b4548f22 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Front-clip harnesses are best for dogs that pull; avoid choke chains and prong collars.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-f5d73ff0689b98df47ddb54ac9ef37f4" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>Front-clip harnesses redirect pulling without causing discomfort</li>

<li>Avoid choke chains and prong collars — they cause pain and damage trust</li>

<li>Measure your dog&#8217;s chest and neck for a proper fit</li>

<li>Padded harnesses reduce rubbing during longer training walks</li>

<li>Y-shaped harnesses allow natural shoulder movement</li>
</ul>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-12c23d5d72083852d8c47c4cbd0d41f8 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:1px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">The right harness transforms walks from a tug-of-war into a pleasant experience. A well-fitted front-clip harness is one of the most effective tools for teaching loose-lead walking, especially when combined with reward-based training.</p>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-white-background-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-2beeb0a415174ef4eb2c4d3c75ede77f" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">📏 Long Lines for Recall Training</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-3be2b1fa43ecbe3269b64dc2cfbbfde8 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Long lines (5-10 metres) are essential for recall training in open spaces.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-3a727d9d6306c47e04662cbf2694d991" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>5-metre lines work well in gardens and enclosed spaces</li>

<li>10-metre lines are ideal for parks and open fields</li>

<li>Choose lightweight, biothane or waterproof material for easy handling</li>

<li>Never use retractable leads for recall training — they teach pulling</li>

<li>Attach to a harness (not a collar) to protect your dog&#8217;s neck</li>
</ul>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-9d92a39f41ecbb277326ff8278e04909 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:1px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">A long line gives your dog the feeling of freedom while keeping you in control. It is the safest way to practise recall in unenclosed areas before your dog is reliable off-lead. Let the line drag rather than holding it taut — this teaches your dog to make good choices independently.</p>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-group has-border-color has-white-background-color has-background is-layout-constrained wp-container-core-group-is-layout-d06944bd wp-block-group-is-layout-constrained" style="border-color:#0073aa;border-width:3px;border-top-left-radius:12px;border-top-right-radius:12px;border-bottom-left-radius:12px;border-bottom-right-radius:12px;margin-bottom:28px;padding-top:28px;padding-right:28px;padding-bottom:28px;padding-left:28px">
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-f471b8e6097c1e66cf0a41a2a7078531" style="color:#0073aa;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:20px;font-style:normal;font-weight:600">🏠 Indoor Training Essentials</h3>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-7180d2ea855d6a029cd5e702d5805ec5 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:1px;margin-bottom:12px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Indoor training tools help with house-training puppies and practising commands in a distraction-free environment.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-c93a50273f48da5bab8bc181e69bfe55" style="color:#333333;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">
<li>Training pads are essential for puppy house-training</li>

<li>Place pads near the door to encourage outdoor transition</li>

<li>Use a house line (short indoor lead) for gentle redirection</li>

<li>Interactive puzzle toys build mental stimulation between sessions</li>

<li>A designated training mat teaches your dog a &#8216;place&#8217; command</li>
</ul>

<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-b0936995b11c6cacd216fb42b52a3462 wp-block-paragraph" style="color:#333333;margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:1px;font-size:16px;line-height:1.6">Training does not stop at the front door. Indoor sessions are just as important as outdoor work, especially for puppies who cannot yet go outside. Short, focused indoor sessions build the foundation for everything your dog will learn later.</p>
</div>
