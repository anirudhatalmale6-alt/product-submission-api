RECOMMENDED OUTREACH PRIORITY ORDER
============================================================

PHASE A - Weeks 1-4: Foundation Building (Tier 3)
Start with Tier 3 targets. These have lower difficulty, faster response
times, and build initial backlink momentum. Success here creates case
studies and content assets useful for higher-tier pitches.

Priority order within Tier 3:
1. Pet event organisers (time-sensitive, tie to upcoming events)
2. Pet podcasts (guest spots create multiple link opportunities)
3. Pet lifestyle bloggers (product evaluation offers)
4. Pet food manufacturers (collaborative content)
5. Industry directories (straightforward submissions)

PHASE B - Weeks 3-8: Authority Partnerships (Tier 2)
Begin Tier 2 outreach once you have 5-8 Tier 3 links secured. University
vet schools and rescue centres value established credibility.

Priority order within Tier 2:
1. Pet forums/communities (immediate engagement, organic)
2. Animal rescue centres (partnership-friendly culture)
3. Industry associations - PFMA data citations (cite first, then pitch)
4. Assistance dog charities (alignment with training content)
5. University vet schools (longest lead time, start early)
6. Established blogs (build relationship before pitching)

PHASE C - Weeks 6-16: Highest Authority (Tier 1)
Approach Tier 1 only after demonstrating quality through Tier 2/3
successes. These targets require polished pitches and proven content
quality.

Priority order within Tier 1:
1. VetCompass / Petplan (data-citation approach, lower barrier)
2. Your Cat Magazine / Dogs Today (editorial pitch, faster turnaround)
3. Cats Protection / Dogs Trust (resource page approach)
4. PDSA / Blue Cross (PAW Report citations, infographic offers)
5. The Kennel Club (breed-specific content partnerships)
6. BVA / RCVS (professional authority, highest bar)
7. RSPCA (most selective, save for last)
8. gov.uk (extremely difficult, opportunistic only)

RISK NOTES - WHAT TO AVOID
============================================================

ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED:
1. No link purchasing or paid placement of any kind
2. No Private Blog Networks (PBNs) or link farms
3. No reciprocal link schemes ("I link to you, you link to me")
4. No comment spam or forum signature links
5. No fake personas or fabricated authority
6. No press release link building services
7. No directory submissions to low-quality or irrelevant directories
8. No article spinning or duplicate content across sites
9. No automated outreach tools that send bulk generic emails
10. No claiming affiliations or credentials that don't exist

QUALITY CONTROL REQUIREMENTS:
- Every target domain must be manually verified as legitimate
- All outreach must be personalised (reference specific content on their site)
- Content offered must genuinely add value to the target's audience
- Never misrepresent PetHub Online's expertise or credentials
- Maintain a maximum of 3 follow-up emails per target (respect no-response)
- Track all outreach in a spreadsheet to avoid duplicate contacts
- Allow minimum 7 days between initial email and first follow-up

GOOGLE PENALTY RISK FACTORS TO MONITOR:
- Anchor text diversity: Never use exact-match anchors more than 10% of total
- Link velocity: Don't acquire more than 8-10 links per month (looks unnatural)
- Relevance signals: All links must come from pet/animal/health related content
- NoFollow balance: A natural profile includes both dofollow and nofollow links
- Geographic consistency: Maintain UK-focused profile (some international is fine)

UK-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE NOTES:
- Comply with UK CAP Code for any claims made in outreach content
- Don't make medical/veterinary claims without professional verification
- Disclose any commercial relationships transparently (ASA guidelines)
- Respect GDPR when collecting contact information for outreach
- Follow UK anti-spam regulations (PECR) for cold outreach emails
